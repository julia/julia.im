Thailand, Part 1

Vacation in paradise

Ko Samui, one of Thailand’s largest islands in the Gulf of Thailand, is known for its palm-fringed beaches circling coconut groves and dense, mountainous rainforest. It’s also home to luxury resorts, posh spas and a rowdy nightlife scene that often attracts a backpacker crowd.

Koh Samui (เกาะสมุย)

The first island we landed at after Bangkok was Koh Samui. It's ideal for a romantic vacation.

Warm sand, palm trees, ocean and fresh air are all the ingredients needed for a perfect summer.

The sunset on the island is incredibly beautiful. If you can stand mosquitoes, go tour the island at night. You won't regret the experience.

Jungles are a big part of Koh Samui's ecosystem. It's fun to ride the motobike through the green paradise! By the way, Thais drive on the left side of the road.

On my birthday I was drinking coconut eggnog and looking at this beautiful island. Can I beat it next year?

The next day we went to see Hin Lat Waterfall running heavily down the mountain rocks.

Hin Lat WaterfallView on map

Nice thoughts over breakfast by the beach...

Koh Tao (เกาะเต่า)

We bought tickets to Koh Tao and hopped on a ferry. It was a fun ride - ice cream, beers and good company.

Photo credit: Dylan Pyle

Koh Tao is an island of fun, partying and diving. We made new friends, visited the Castle Party and drank enormous amounts of coconut cocktails of all kinds, colors and tastes. Good times!

We were hiking, diving, snorkeling, riding the motobike, lying on the hot sand under the generous Thai sun during the day and partying as the moon came up.

But that's not all the adventures we had. Stay tuned for Part 2 of my Thailand story!
