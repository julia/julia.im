April 2015

Sonoma, CA

Camping weekend with Twitter design team

Sonoma is the southwestern county and largest producer of California's Wine Country region, which also includes Napa, Mendocino, and Lake counties. It has 13 approved American Viticultural Areas and over 250 wineries. In 2002, Sonoma County ranked as the 32nd county in the United States in agricultural production.

Afternoon ride to Sonoma

Friday after work I raced home to finish packing, grab my Canon and jump into the car. It was also the day when Periscope came out so we were enjoying riding, laughing and periscoping on the way to Sonoma.

This is how San Francisco looks like right after you leave Golden Gate bridge. White LEGO blocks the over ocean.

At some point on a way to Sonoma you find yourself looking at country landscapes. Peaceful life of Northern California: farms, horses and strawberry fields.

At the campfire

Shortly after we arrived.

Adi and Mike were already there, so were DBells, Tash and Erin. They were setting up the tent. We brought a bunch of food and beer with us and started unpacking.

It started to get dark very quickly.

The campfire is what this all about. It feels so warm and welcoming sitting around the fire, having nice chat and drinking cold beer and wine.

We had such a good time. The stars...

Kalani played guitar...

We were having lots of fun.

Day 2

Early in the morning the camp looks like a field of mushrooms. Silent.

Everybody started to wake up slowly and thinking about breakfast. Good thing we had Jason with us and he came fully prepared for the most amazing camping breakfast you can possible imagine.

We made fresh drip coffee. Also had some visitors.

After breakfast we went hiking on Planet Trail and made it to the very top while other folks went wine tasting.

We experienced beautful views of Sonoma and apparently made all the way through the Galaxy.

Met the same riders in the forest.

Sonoma, California.

Later that afternoon more folks joned us and we were making s'mores, had silent disco and signing songs.

Day 3. Rain

We made it home early, wet and happy and spent the rest of the weekend indoors with hot tea and good memories.
