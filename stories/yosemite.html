August 2014

Yosemite, CA

Hiking in a beautiful national park

Yosemite National Park is a United States National Park spanning eastern portions of Tuolumne, Mariposa and Madera counties in the central eastern portion of the U.S. state of California.

Address: Yosemite Village, CA 95389

Area: 1,190 sq miles (3,081 km2)

Established: October 1, 1890

On the road

It was a sunny August day when we hit the road. We took 99 highway as the fastest way to get to the place. We stayed in little tourist city called Mariposa.

Mariposa, CAView on map

Hiking to the top

Early in the morning we drove to the park, prepared for a long hike.

The first thing that strikes - the air is unbelievably fresh, you simply can't get enough of it.

We started climbing to the top. It was super hot, fun and enjoyable hike. We had so much fun!

Right from the start we experienced breathtaking views of Yosemite - rocks, mountains, trees, fields, fog.

Yosemite is incredible.

Fed some wild animals on the way to the top.

It feels like standing on the edge of the world, being so tiny and...

Yosemite is famous for being a home to Giant Sequoias 300 feet tall and two centuries old.

Sunset in Yosemite

We came down right by the time of the sunset and caught up Yosemite at beautiful golden hour.

The colors are so rich and vibrant that you feel like you are swimming in light. You stand and stare at magnificent beauty.

Pure gold.

We drove back to the village we were staying at and had a nice and relaxing evening. Climbing the mountains is exausting activity after all. We were pretty tired but super happy.

Road back

I was looking forward for photographing wind mills for quite some time and got so excited when we saw them on the way.

Wind mills are beautiful.

More californian scenery.
